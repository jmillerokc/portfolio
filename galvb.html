Key Insight
Steady Stars look stable but have shrunk 16% since 2018 (1,153 → 965 weeks). They're paying full freight for declining volume. Ramp Ups are the only category with double-digit growth (+28%) at acceptable CAC ($124). New sites cost $190 to acquire, nearly 2× a High Flyer, which is the cost of being early in the curve.
03 / Proposal

Reallocation, with total spend held flat.

Cut 15% from the four lowest-efficiency buckets and redistribute to the three highest. Same $2.02M envelope, working harder.

Takeaway
Chicago Under Performers lose $44K (16 sites at -15%), SoCal Under Performers lose $54K. That money funds Bay Area Ramp Ups (+60%, where 25% growth is already happening at $94 CAC) and Denver's lone Ramp Up (+150%, at 65% growth and $108 CAC). The reallocation moves dollars toward where the math is already proving itself.
